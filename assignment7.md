Only 14% of AUPD cases resulted in serious administrative action or arrest in 2017, according to new data obtained from the department.
After reviewing crime logs provided by the American University Police Department from 2017 through the first semester of 2026, our investigation found that 103 police reports resulted in arrest, referral
to the university's Title IX office, or referral to the Metro Police Department. That number represents just 14.5% of the 710 cases that the 
university police department handled that year. 
The majority of cases handled that year, 257, were still classified as 'pending' in the documents we reviewed for this report. At this time, the department
has not responded to requests for clarification on why cases from prior years were still labeled as 'pending'. It was not immediately clear what other designations, such as 'SCCRS Ref', which 
accounted for 155 cases that year, referred to. We are continuing to reach out to AUPD for further clarification. The data also showed that a large number of reports resulted in 
no complaint being filed (59), no suspect or witnesses identified (33), and victim declinations (8). 
Our initial analysis of the data has uncovered a number of trends related to the types of crimes prevalent on campus, as well as which areas have the highest concentrations of incidents. At this time, we are still seeking additional information from the university, as well as logs from the year 2024, which have not yet been provided. Our reporting looks to analyze over 2860 campus police reports to better inform the AU community and promote safety campus-wide. We will continue to update our reporting as we analyze the data and find more trends.
