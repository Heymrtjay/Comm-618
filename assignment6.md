The two charts show information compiled from AUPD Crime logs in 2017. In the first chart, "vs offense", we see that, somewhat as expected, a large majority of campus crimes involve alcohol and drug violations, and theft. When you remove those outliers, however, we see that burglary, sexual abuse, vandalism, graffiti, and harassment all occur at about the same rate on campus. Further analysis is required to see where the crimes overlap and potentially uncover other trends. However, the main takeaway is that property crimes led as the most likely crime on campus in 2017. Chart 2 shows outcomes from incident reports, and chart two shows crimes that were committed and logged. In chart one, we see that only a small number of crimes actually lead to arrest. Far more result in no complaint being filed. What is most interesting is that the two most likely outcomes, SCCRS Ref and 'Pending', are undefined. There is no information about what an SCCRS Ref is, and so far, AUPD has not returned our request for comment. The Pending column is much more concerning, especially since this data is from 2017. There is no indication that a large majority of cases are ever solved. In fact, the most likely outcome is for the case to move to 'pending' status, followed by SCCRS ref, no complaint filed, or MPD refferal. About 5% of cases end in arrest, nearly the same amount as the number of cases with no suspect or witnesses.
