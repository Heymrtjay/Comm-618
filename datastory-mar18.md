This article discusses the rising number of US citizens moving abroad.

https://www.wsj.com/us-news/americans-leaving-the-us-migration-a5795bfa?mod=hp_lead_pos8

The first chart shows that in-migration and net migration overall have been declining, though the tail of the graphs are somewhat confusing. The article also notes that official US data doesn't fully capture the number of people leaving.
The next three charts show the number of US citizens in Portugal, the number moving Ireland, and the amount of applications for French residency over the last 10 years or less, depending on the graph. Each set of information was provided by the receiving country.
The final graph shows two pieces of information; the number of Norwegian born residents in the US, and the number of US born residents in Norway. Its particularly interesting because, as noted in the article, Norway is one of the countries often mentioned by the President as a desired source of immigrants.
One particular problem with the data provided in that in each of the data sets provided by Ireland and France, there is at least one year of missing data, however that is not really reflected in the graph in any meaningful way.
Also, though it mentions it in the text of the article, I think vusually it would have benefited from at least one graph or chart comparing the cost of living in one of the destination countries, as that is very much a factor in these relocations.
