The biggest difference is, the first story is much more in depth and compares a number of different measures. Article #2 only shows the unemployment rate,
and the consumer price index. It gives a much less detailed analysis of the individual factors that contribute to the state of the economy.
In contrast, the first article provides data on non-farm employment, total unemployment, inflation, consumer prices on coffee, ground beef, electricity, and gasoline.
The article also shows data on wage growth, stock market performance, the trade deficit, manufacturing, and housing affordability. Not only that, this article gives context and nuance to the numbers in a way that the second article does not. 

Overall, I would have liked more analysis in the second article, and on the wage growth graph in the first article I think it would have been useful to display the data a different way.
