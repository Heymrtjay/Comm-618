This is an older dataset from the Department of Data at WaPo. I stumbled on this dataset 
because it was linked in a more recent story about civil war leadership, which was a follow-up to reporting about how frontline leaders are disappearing from the workplace.

https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/03/02/leadership-traits-frontline-managers-civil-war/

The dataset is about interactions between birds at a bird feeder. The most interesting thing about this dataset 
is that its driven by a site called feederwatch.org, which is a nation-wide community of bird watchers who contribute observations about birds in their Communities. The site contains a number of different data presentations, to include distribution trends and flock size of specific birds by species. At the time of the article, it was said to have nearly 100,000 bird interactions, in November of 2021. The site also contains a number of raw datasets that can be downloaded and reviewed.

https://feederwatch.org/explore/raw-dataset-requests/
